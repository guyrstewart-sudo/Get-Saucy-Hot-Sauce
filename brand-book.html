01 — The Brand

Our Story

Get Saucy is craft hot sauce from Asheville, North Carolina, built on one belief: hot sauce should taste like something — not just burn. So we grow our own peppers, roast and balance every batch by hand, and bottle in small runs. Scotch bonnet, ghost, reaper, habanero — heat with a backbone.

But the sauce is only half the story. Every flavor is a collaboration with a local artist, who designs original art for the label. Buy a bottle, support the scene. Our breakout, Strabanero — strawberry and habanero — wears artwork by @mossy.tats. That's the whole idea: supporting local artists through hot sauce, one bottle at a time.

We ship nationwide, and we cook, too — the chef behind the sauce takes private-dinner bookings, bringing Get Saucy straight to the table.

02 — North Star

Mission & Positioning

Mission

Make small-batch craft hot sauce that earns its place at the table — bold, balanced, garden-grown — and lift up local artists with every label we print.

Vision

A world where "spicy" never means "one-note," where a bottle of sauce can also be a piece of art and a hand extended to a local maker.

Positioning

For flavor-driven home cooks and hosts who think most hot sauce is all heat and no soul, Get Saucy is small-batch, garden-grown craft hot sauce from Asheville — every flavor wrapped in original art by a local artist. Unlike mass-market bottles built for shock value and shelf life, Get Saucy is built for taste first, community always.

05 — Visual Identity

Logo

The mark is a bubble-graffiti GET SAUCY wordmark in gold on a teal disc, with a single sauce drip — street-art energy, craft heart. Use the disc version as the primary mark (avatar, favicon, label seal). Give it room to breathe; never stretch or recolor the gold.

Note: This is a clean digital recreation of the real graffiti mark for web/favicon use. For print/labels, keep the original hand-drawn artist version as the hero lockup.

Pairing rule

08 — Look & Feel

Packaging & Photography

Packaging

  • Classic woozy bottle, clear glass — let the sauce color sell itself.
  • Original artist label per flavor; credit the artist on the back.
  • Consistent front-of-bottle heat meter (1–5 peppers).
  • Black tamper cap; gold "GET SAUCY" graffiti mark recurring across the family.

Photography

  • Sauce is the hero — warm light, charcoal/teal backdrops, a deliberate drip.
  • Real food, mid-action: the pour, the dunk, the glistening wing.
  • Garden & harvest shots — peppers on the plant, in the colander.
  • Hands at work: roasting, bottling, plating. Show the craft.
  • Lifestyle = the table: candlelight, full plates, people leaning in.
